Element 01 — Upper Field
The City Skyline

Three crimson building silhouettes rise above the frame — a dominant central tower flanked by smaller structures. This skyline represents the full spectrum of South Florida properties ESi certifies: from a small four-unit multifamily to a large commercial complex. The crimson signals urgency, accountability, and the weight of public safety.

Element 02 — The Frame
The Architectural Frame

Two angular structural pillars extend outward at the base of the skyline, forming the profile of a building entrance or structural elevation. The frame creates a defined inspection zone — and gives the mark a grounded, physical presence. It tells you immediately: this is a firm that works inside buildings.

Element 03 — The Centerpiece
The Certification Shield

A shield with a checkmark sits at the junction of skyline and frame — the heart of the mark. This is Oscar's essential addition: the shield communicates certification authority, the checkmark communicates approval. Together they say what no tagline ever could: ESi has been here, has inspected this, and the building is cleared.

Element 04 — The Lettermark
The ESi Monogram

The geometric ESi lettermark sits inside the frame in midnight navy — the identity's text anchor. Uppercase E and S in bold geometric weight, lowercase i with a diagonal slash. The capitalization is proprietary: E·S·i. The diagonal cut through the letterforms reflects the precision of architectural and structural draftsmanship.

Hierarchy & Intent

Deep Crimson leads. It appears in every primary application — the architectural frame, the skyline, every heading that demands authority. It is the color of accountability and urgency.

Midnight Navy grounds. It appears in the ESi lettermark, the shield outline, body text, and all secondary system elements. It is the color of expertise and depth.

These two colors and only these two constitute the ESi brand palette. No variants, no tints, no additions without explicit approval.

Approved Combinations

● Crimson on White — primary brand pairing across all print and digital

● Navy on White — secondary pairing for body text, data, and UI

● White on Crimson — reverse CTAs and highlight banners only

● White on Navy — reversed and dark-field applications

● Crimson on Navy — high-impact headers on dark backgrounds

06
Usage Guidelines

Use It Right.
Every Time.

✓
Always Do
  • ✓Use approved production files only — never redraw, recreate, or screenshot the mark
  • ✓Preserve "ESi" exactly — uppercase E, uppercase S, lowercase i — in all materials
  • ✓Maintain clear space equal to the "E" cap-height on all four sides at all times
  • ✓Use Group 02 (black silhouette) for all single-color print applications
  • ✓Scale proportionally using a corner handle — never stretch or compress independently
  • ✓Use SVG or EPS vector files for all production and commercial print output
  • ✓Use Group 03 (white versions) on all dark-field backgrounds
✕
Never Do
  • ✕Never add drop shadows, outer glows, gradients, bevels, or emboss effects
  • ✕Never place the mark on busy photographic or patterned backgrounds
  • ✕Never alter any color within the logo — two approved colors only, no variants
  • ✕Never rotate, skew, warp, or apply perspective transforms to the logo
  • ✕Never write "ESI", "Esi", "esi", or "E.S.I." — only "ESi" is correct
  • ✕Never use a 3D render as a production or official print file
  • ✕Never add taglines or extra copy as part of the logo mark itself
