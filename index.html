The Concept
A Mark Built on
Structure & Trust

Protection
Through
Precision

"21 years of engineering expertise behind every report."

When a building owner receives an official government compliance notice — a 40-year recertification, a milestone inspection, a structural integrity mandate — they are not searching for a vendor. They are searching for a partner they can trust with the physical safety of the people inside their building.

The shield mark is the cornerstone of this identity. It carries the weight of that promise in its most universally understood form: a symbol that says, unambiguously, that what it covers is certified, verified, and safe. Within it, three zones tell the complete story of ESi's expertise.

The wordmark "Engineering Services, inc." is set in a bold condensed construction that mirrors the vertical confidence of South Florida's skyline — direct, structural, and without ornament. Together, mark and wordmark form a system that is immediately authoritative and deeply local.

Two colors. No excess. Deep crimson signals urgency, accountability, and authority. Institutional blue anchors expertise, trust, and civic responsibility. The combination owns a clear and unoccupied space in the South Florida professional services market.

03
Mark Anatomy
Zone 01 — Upper Field
The Skyline

A South Florida cityscape silhouette in institutional blue. Every building in that skyline is a building that must be recertified — grounding the mark in a specific, real geography while communicating the full scope of ESi's domain.

Zone 02 — Center Band
The Lettermark

The "ESi" monogram — E and S uppercase, i lowercase — rendered against deep crimson. This deliberate capitalization is a proprietary typographic decision preserved across all applications. The red band is the identity's highest-contrast anchor.

Zone 03 — Lower Field
The Infrastructure

An arch bridge with water lines in institutional blue — referencing South Florida's infrastructure legacy, its waterfront character, and ESi's guardrail and waterfront inspection capability: the service most competitors never mention.

The Outer Form
The Shield

A classic heraldic shield with a pointed base — a universal symbol of protection, certification, and authority. In the context of building safety compliance, no form communicates the brand promise more efficiently or more immediately.

Hierarchy Rules

Deep Crimson is the dominant brand color. It must appear in every primary lockup, all primary CTAs, and all marketing materials as the leading accent.

Institutional Blue is the secondary color. Reserved for the mark's interior zones, icons, data callouts, and supporting UI elements.

Near Black replaces pure #000 for all text. Pure White is the universal background — no tinted surfaces in the core system.

Approved Combinations

● Crimson on White · Primary brand pairing, headings, CTAs

● Blue on White · Informational, data, links, secondary UI

● White on Near-Black · Reversed / dark-mode applications

● White on Crimson · Reverse CTA buttons only

05
Logo Variations
Var. 01 — Primary Horizontal Lockup
Full Color · Horizontal · Transparent

The canonical logo. Website header, email signatures, proposals, letterhead. When in doubt, use this version.

Var. 02 — Standalone Mark
Shield · Icon Only

App icon, social profile, favicon, embroidery. Never replaces the primary lockup in formal materials.

Var. 03 — Wordmark Only
Wordmark · Stacked

Large-format banners, co-branding, or when the shield appears separately elsewhere in the layout.

Var. 04 — Monochrome
Monochrome · Black

Single-color print: embossing, letterpress, fax, newspapers, black-and-white forms.

Var. 05 — Dimensional
3D Render · Presentation

Proposal covers, video intros, premium marketing. Decorative only — never replaces the flat vector master.

06
Usage Guidelines
✓
Always Do
  • ✓Use approved logo files only — never redraw or recreate the mark
  • ✓Preserve "ESi": uppercase E, uppercase S, lowercase i — always
  • ✓Maintain clear space equal to the "E" cap-height on all sides
  • ✓Use the monochrome version for single-color print applications
  • ✓Scale proportionally from a corner handle — never stretch
  • ✓Use near-black (#0e0e0e) for all body text — not pure black
  • ✓Minimum: 120px screen · 1 inch print for the full lockup
✕
Never Do
  • ✕Never add drop shadows, outer glows, or emboss effects
  • ✕Never place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds
  • ✕Never alter any color within the logo to an unapproved color
  • ✕Never rotate, skew, or apply perspective transforms
  • ✕Never write "ESI", "Esi", or "esi" — always "ESi"
  • ✕Never use the 3D render as a production or print logo file
  • ✕Never add taglines or extra copy adjacent to the mark
